Anyone can take part in virtual tours!

Several museums offer virtual tours for visually impaired people.

Ways for the visually impaired to take part in virtual tours

  • Audio guides and audio descriptive displays come alive for everyone. Classic examples the Spanish Museum for the Blind, the National Museum of Natural History and the National Yellowstone Park.
  • Ability to copy associated webpages and relevant material into word or any other document type available for printing and of course convertible to braille. That way the visitors are able to follow the tour while captioning on all that is described, as in the NASA.
  • Tactile experiences and 3D printing and the use of multisensory displays appealing to sight, sound, touch and smell. The V&A museum as well as the Smithsonian Museum run numerous events focusing on a variety of collections, thus encouraging people to repeat their visit.
